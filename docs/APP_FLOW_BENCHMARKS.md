# App Flow Benchmarks

This document defines what we borrow from established learning products as principles. We are not cloning their brands, UI, content, or copyrighted flows. We are adapting proven learning mechanics into a Kyrgyz-specific, adult, mobile-first experience.

## Benchmarking Rules

- Borrow principles, not content or visual identity.
- Keep the app premium, practical, and adult.
- Avoid overpressure, clutter, and childish gamification.
- Tie every flow to Kyrgyz learning goals.
- Keep methodology and source metadata hidden from normal learner UI.

## Duolingo Principles

Useful principles:

- Bite-sized lessons.
- Path progression.
- Daily habit loop.
- Instant feedback.
- Review weak spots.
- Clear next action.

What to avoid:

- Overly childish tone.
- Excessive pressure.
- Over-gamified screens that distract from learning.
- Random-feeling practice disconnected from the current lesson.

Kyrgyz adaptation:

- Short lessons stay focused on survival communication and reading confidence.
- Practice uses lesson vocabulary and grammar.
- Review queue returns learners to missed items.
- Progress is visible but not stressful.

## HSK Books Principles

Useful principles:

- Level structure.
- Controlled vocabulary.
- Dialogue/text before grammar.
- Grammar or language point after usage.
- Workbook-style practice.
- Unit review and staged tests.
- Teacher-guide logic behind the scenes.

What to avoid:

- Copying HSK content.
- Treating HSK as a language-specific source for Kyrgyz.
- Long textbook pages in a mobile app.

Kyrgyz adaptation:

- Use Level -> Unit -> Lesson progression.
- Keep one main grammar point per lesson.
- Put Kyrgyz dialogue/text before explanation.
- Use practice immediately after explanation.
- Keep review aligned to Кыргызтест/local references where possible.

## Memrise Principles

Useful principles:

- Native-speaker audio/video inspiration.
- Real-world phrase exposure.
- Repetition of useful phrases.
- Memory support through repeated encounters.

What to avoid:

- Presenting unreviewed or synthetic audio as native quality.
- Adding media without a clear learning job.

Kyrgyz adaptation:

- Prioritize human-recorded Kyrgyz audio for vocabulary, phrases, and dialogues.
- Use common everyday phrases before abstract grammar.
- Let audio support pronunciation and listening without overclaiming speech evaluation.

## Busuu Principles

Useful principles:

- Study plan.
- Goal-based onboarding.
- Compact lessons.
- Future community or native review inspiration.

What to avoid:

- Overcomplicated onboarding before the learner can start.
- Social/community features before moderation and quality standards exist.

Kyrgyz adaptation:

- Onboarding should choose RU -> KY, EN -> KY, or KY -> KY.
- Learner goals should shape starting recommendations.
- Community/native review can be considered later for writing or speaking, after safety and review processes exist.

## LingQ Principles

Useful principles:

- Graded reading/listening.
- Learning vocabulary in context.
- Reading library inspiration.
- Tracking known and unknown words.

What to avoid:

- Long authentic texts too early.
- Importing copyrighted readings without permission.
- Turning K0/K1 into a reading-heavy experience too soon.

Kyrgyz adaptation:

- K0 uses micro-reading.
- K1 uses short original everyday texts.
- K2-K5 gradually introduce adapted stories, culture/history texts, public language, and literature.
- Every reading has source and rights notes internally.

## Anki Principles

Useful principles:

- Flashcards.
- Spaced repetition.
- Focus on weak material.
- Learner-controlled review.

What to avoid:

- Exposing too much scheduling complexity.
- Making the whole app feel like a card deck.
- Inventing SRS manually without reason.

Kyrgyz adaptation:

- Flashcards should come from lesson vocabulary, phrases, grammar, and mistakes.
- Missed answers should become review material.
- Use FSRS or another proven implementation rather than custom scheduling.

## Our Kyrgyz-specific Flow

Default daily flow:

1. Open app.
2. See next lesson or review queue.
3. Continue a short lesson or fix weak spots.
4. Practice with instant feedback.
5. Review missed items.
6. Save progress.
7. Later, flashcards reinforce weak material.

Default lesson flow:

1. Story.
2. Goals.
3. Vocabulary.
4. Dialogue or text.
5. Breakdown.
6. Grammar.
7. Practice.
8. Mini-game.
9. Speaking.
10. AI roleplay.
11. Review.

Default review flow:

1. Show what needs attention.
2. Let learner retry directly when supported.
3. Mark corrected items.
4. Later generate flashcards and SRS schedule.

## Product Standard

A borrowed principle is acceptable only if it:

- Makes Kyrgyz learning easier.
- Fits mobile-first UX.
- Keeps lessons short and structured.
- Supports adult learners.
- Can be tested.
- Does not introduce legal or content-quality risk.
