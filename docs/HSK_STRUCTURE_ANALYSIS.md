# HSK Structure Analysis

This product is HSK-inspired in structure only. HSK books and materials may inform methodology, but their learner-facing content must not be copied into this app.

## What We Borrow From HSK-style Methodology

We borrow:

- Level-based progression.
- Textbook + workbook + teacher-guide logic.
- Predictable lesson sequence.
- Controlled vocabulary.
- Dialogues before grammar.
- Grammar/language points introduced gradually.
- Exercises immediately after explanation.
- Review and testing after units.
- Audio/listening support.
- Practical communication topics.
- Staged assessment.

We do not borrow:

- HSK dialogues.
- HSK vocabulary lists as fixed content.
- HSK grammar explanations.
- HSK exercises.
- HSK audio scripts.
- HSK tests.

## Adapted Learning Structure

Our adapted structure is:

Level -> Unit -> Lesson -> Vocabulary -> Dialogue/Text -> Breakdown -> Grammar Point -> Practice -> Mini-game -> Speaking/AI Roleplay -> Review

This differs from a generic website because every lesson is a short app session with:

- Controlled input.
- One main outcome.
- Immediate practice.
- A lightweight reinforcement loop.
- Progress tracking.
- Reviewable content metadata.

## HSK-inspired Lesson Components

Each app lesson may adapt these structural components:

- Lesson title.
- Communication goal.
- Vocabulary list.
- Dialogue or reading text.
- Phrase breakdown.
- Language/grammar point.
- Workbook-style exercises.
- Listening task.
- Speaking task.
- Writing/reconstruction task.
- Review test.

## Product Adaptation

For Kyrgyz, each component must be adapted through:

- Kyrgyz grammar validation.
- Kyrgyztest/local level alignment.
- Kyrgyz-as-foreign-language methodist review.
- Mobile-first UX constraints.
- Original or rights-cleared content creation.

## Component Mapping

Lesson title:

- HSK-style function: identifies the situation or learning goal.
- Kyrgyz app adaptation: short mobile title tied to one unit and level.

Communication goal:

- HSK-style function: tells the learner what they can do after the lesson.
- Kyrgyz app adaptation: can-do statement connected to practice and review.

Vocabulary list:

- HSK-style function: controlled new words before or near the lesson text.
- Kyrgyz app adaptation: small level-safe list with source notes, validation status, translations, and future audio.

Dialogue or reading text:

- HSK-style function: target language in context.
- Kyrgyz app adaptation: original or rights-cleared Kyrgyz input, reviewed for naturalness and level.

Phrase breakdown:

- HSK-style function: helps learners notice chunks and reusable patterns.
- Kyrgyz app adaptation: track-specific breakdown for RU -> KY, EN -> KY, and KY -> KY.

Language/grammar point:

- HSK-style function: introduces one or more language points after input.
- Kyrgyz app adaptation: one main grammar point per lesson, validated against approved Kyrgyz references.

Workbook-style exercises:

- HSK-style function: immediate controlled practice.
- Kyrgyz app adaptation: schema-driven practice, mobile-first interactions, testable answers.

Listening task:

- HSK-style function: audio reinforcement and comprehension.
- Kyrgyz app adaptation: placeholder in MVP; later native-speaker audio with QA.

Speaking task:

- HSK-style function: guided production.
- Kyrgyz app adaptation: short prompt, allowed phrases, optional AI roleplay later.

Writing/reconstruction task:

- HSK-style function: rebuild language forms and reinforce structure.
- Kyrgyz app adaptation: word order, sentence reconstruction, or short typing tasks after the content is validated.

Review test:

- HSK-style function: unit-level consolidation and assessment.
- Kyrgyz app adaptation: unit review, spaced review, local progress now, account-backed progress later.

## Staged Assessment

Assessment should grow by level:

- K0: recognition, sound/letter matching, micro-reading.
- K1: recognition plus controlled phrase production.
- K2: short comprehension and reconstruction.
- K3: connected reading and guided output.
- K4: semi-authentic comprehension and functional production.
- K5: extended reading, argumentation, stylistic control, and public speaking.

All assessment design must remain aligned with source methodology and methodist review.
