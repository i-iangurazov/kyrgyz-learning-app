# User Journeys

This document defines the primary MVP personas and their expected product journeys.

## Persona A: RU -> KY Beginner

Profile:

- Russian-speaking person in Kyrgyzstan.
- Wants to finally speak basic Kyrgyz in daily life.
- May have seen Kyrgyz in school or public spaces but does not feel confident using it.

Motivation:

- Speak respectfully in everyday situations.
- Understand community and service interactions.
- Feel less dependent on Russian.
- Use Kyrgyz at work, in public offices, with neighbors, or with family/community.

Pain points:

- Fear of making mistakes.
- Grammar feels unfamiliar.
- Existing materials may feel too academic or school-like.
- Learner may know isolated words but cannot build phrases.
- Pronunciation and Kyrgyz-specific letters may feel intimidating.

Onboarding path:

- Choose RU -> KY.
- Choose goal such as speak in daily life, work/customer service, or understand family/community.
- Choose current level.
- Placement test recommended but skippable.

First session:

- Start at K0 or K1 depending on reading confidence.
- Complete a short greeting or introduction lesson.
- See Russian support but Kyrgyz remains visually primary.
- End with one usable phrase.

Daily session:

- Open Home.
- Continue the next lesson or review.
- Spend 8-12 minutes.
- Practice recognition, short phrases, and simple output.

Lesson expectations:

- Clear Russian explanations.
- Practical Kyrgyz examples.
- Short dialogues.
- Gentle correction.
- No dense grammar walls.

What makes them return:

- Feeling able to use one phrase today.
- Progress that feels visible but not stressful.
- Lessons connected to real daily life.
- Review that makes old words easier.

What could make them quit:

- Too much grammar terminology too early.
- Feeling judged for mistakes.
- Long lessons.
- Kyrgyz that sounds unnatural or unvalidated.
- Russian explanations that are confusing or too literal.

## Persona B: KY -> KY Improver

Profile:

- Kyrgyz speaker or heritage speaker.
- Understands Kyrgyz but wants better grammar, vocabulary, reading, and writing.
- May speak informally but lacks confidence in standard written language.

Motivation:

- Improve literacy.
- Strengthen grammar.
- Read better.
- Write more correctly.
- Speak with more precision in school, work, or public contexts.

Pain points:

- Beginner foreign-language material can feel too basic.
- Grammar explanations may be too Russian- or English-centered.
- Learner may not want translations.
- Reading may jump too quickly from simple text to literature.

Onboarding path:

- Choose KY -> KY.
- Choose improve grammar/literacy, school/university, or work/customer service.
- Choose current level such as understand but cannot speak, speak a little, or improve literacy.
- Placement should detect reading and grammar gaps.

First session:

- Start with a diagnostic or a literacy-focused lesson.
- Use Kyrgyz explanations where possible.
- Offer simple but respectful tasks.

Daily session:

- Continue reading, vocabulary, grammar, or writing-focused practice.
- Review weak areas.
- See progress in can-do statements, not just XP.

Lesson expectations:

- Kyrgyz-first explanations.
- More reading and writing over time.
- Grammar clarity without patronizing tone.
- Cultural and literary progression later.

What makes them return:

- Feeling that the app respects their existing Kyrgyz.
- Visible improvement in correctness and reading confidence.
- Useful literacy practice.
- Graded readings that are not childish.

What could make them quit:

- Treating them like an absolute foreign beginner.
- Too much translation.
- Overly simplistic examples.
- Unnatural Kyrgyz.
- Lack of reading/literacy path.

## Persona C: EN -> KY Learner

Profile:

- Foreigner, expat, tourist, researcher, volunteer, or worker.
- Wants practical Kyrgyz for daily interactions.
- May know little about Cyrillic or Turkic-language structure.

Motivation:

- Greet people politely.
- Navigate food, shops, taxis, directions, and community interactions.
- Understand basic signs and phrases.
- Build confidence living or traveling in Kyrgyzstan.

Pain points:

- Cyrillic and Kyrgyz-specific letters are new.
- Few accessible Kyrgyz learning resources exist.
- Grammar patterns may feel unfamiliar from English.
- Needs practical language quickly.

Onboarding path:

- Choose EN -> KY.
- Choose travel, speak in daily life, work/customer service, or school/university.
- Choose current level.
- Placement test can be skipped for true beginners.

First session:

- Begin with K0 alphabet/greeting work or K1 survival phrases if they can read Cyrillic.
- Keep English explanations short.
- Show Kyrgyz visually first with support below.

Daily session:

- Continue short practical lessons.
- Use audio and speaking once available.
- Practice survival scenarios.

Lesson expectations:

- Clear English explanations.
- Practical examples.
- Reading support.
- Transliteration only where methodist-approved and useful.
- Gentle introduction to Cyrillic.

What makes them return:

- Useful phrases they can say immediately.
- Short daily progress.
- Clear path through alphabet and survival Kyrgyz.
- Safe roleplay for real situations.

What could make them quit:

- Too much Cyrillic too quickly without support.
- Long grammar explanations.
- Lack of pronunciation/audio support.
- Lessons that feel academic rather than practical.
- Unclear progress path.

## Cross-persona Product Requirements

All personas need:

- A clear track.
- A clear starting point.
- Short sessions.
- Mobile-first interaction.
- Original and validated Kyrgyz content.
- Respectful correction.
- Progress without pressure.
- A reason to return tomorrow.
