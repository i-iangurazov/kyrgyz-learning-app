# Content Quality Reset

This document protects the MVP from treating prototype content as final curriculum.

## Current Seed Content Status

- Current K0/K1 seed lessons are prototype samples only.
- Seed lessons exist to test the learning flow, schema, practice engine, audio placeholders, and database mapping.
- Seed lessons must remain internally marked as demo content, not methodist reviewed, and not linguistically final.
- The learner UI must not show raw demo, source, rights, schema, or review metadata.
- No sample grammar, translation, pronunciation, or usage note should be presented internally as authoritative final content.

## Real Lesson Standard

Real lessons must be created through the documented methodology:

1. Use approved source methodology and level sequencing.
2. Select vocabulary, grammar, and themes appropriate to K0/K1.
3. Write original Kyrgyz dialogues, texts, examples, and exercises.
4. Record or attach audio only when rights and review status are clear.
5. Validate all Kyrgyz language, grammar, translations, register, and naturalness with a Kyrgyz methodist/linguist.
6. Keep source notes, rights notes, and review status internally.
7. Publish only after approval.

## Copyright And Source Rules

Allowed:

- Original app-authored learning content.
- Public-domain or licensed material when rights are documented.
- Source materials used for methodology, sequencing, validation, and theme planning.
- Adapted/original texts that are reviewed and rights-safe.

Not allowed:

- Copying protected textbook dialogues, exercises, grammar explanations, or reading passages.
- Scraping copyrighted course material into seed data.
- Light paraphrases of protected textbook content.
- Presenting unreviewed demo content as finished Kyrgyz curriculum.

## Trust Rule

The product should feel polished to learners while the content pipeline remains honest internally. Hide production metadata from learners, but never remove internal review markers until a qualified reviewer approves the content.
