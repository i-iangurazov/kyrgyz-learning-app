## 2. Current Risks

### Kyrgyz Naturalness Risks

- `Жакшы?` may be too elliptical or unnatural as a standalone beginner greeting prompt.
- `Мен жакшы.` may not naturally express the intended meaning.
- `Сен жакшы.` may be unnatural or misleading as an example for `сен`.
- The dialogue may feel like a translated teaching sketch rather than natural spoken Kyrgyz.

### Learning Objective Risks

- The lesson currently mixes greeting, thanks, `жакшы`, and `сен` without a clearly validated first-use conversation model.
- The objective may be too broad for the first lesson if formal/informal greeting choice is unresolved.
- `сен` may introduce register and pronoun choices before the learner has enough context.

### Grammar Sequencing Risks

- "Short replies" may be better treated as a language note or phrase pattern rather than a grammar point.
- The lesson should avoid presenting any grammar as authoritative before methodist review.
- If the revised lesson introduces formal/informal distinction, that could become too much for K0 lesson 1.

### Vocabulary Selection Risks

- The vocabulary set may be incomplete if the natural first dialogue requires a different greeting or response.
- `Сен` may not be necessary for the first lesson.
- `Жакшы` may need a more natural phrase context before learners practice it.

### Translation Risks

- RU translation `Привет! Хорошо?` for `Салам! Жакшы?` is literal and may not convey the intended social meaning.
- RU translation `У меня хорошо` for `Мен жакшы.` may be unnatural.
- EN translation `You are good` for `Сен жакшы.` may mislead future EN learners.

### Audio / TTS Risks

- This is the first learner contact with Kyrgyz audio, so pronunciation quality matters disproportionately.
- Placeholder/TTS audio must not be presented as final or native-quality.
- The reviewer must confirm transcripts before any recording or synthetic voice comparison is used for beta.

### Source Grounding Risks

- Current content is original demo content but not yet checked against beginner Kyrgyz-as-foreign-language sequencing.
- No source grants permission to copy content; source use must remain methodology/reference/adaptation only.
- The lesson needs native speaker/methodist judgment more than grammar-book citation alone because greeting naturalness and register are pragmatic.

## 3. Proposed Target For Revised Lesson

`k0-u1-l1` should become the first clean beginner lesson for RU -> KY learners.

Target direction:

- Teach the simplest safe greeting and one simplest response.
- Keep the lesson focused on practical recognition and controlled use.
- Use natural Kyrgyz that a methodist/native speaker would accept.
- Avoid overcomplicated grammar.
- Avoid formal/informal explanation unless the methodist says it is essential from lesson 1.
- Use a very short dialogue that sounds real.
- Use one micro-reading only if it reinforces the same phrase set.
- Keep exercises directly tied to the vocabulary and dialogue.
- Keep all content original or explicitly licensed; do not copy textbook dialogues or exercises.

Preferred learner outcome:

- The learner can recognize a first greeting.
- The learner can recognize or say one short response.
- The learner can understand `Рахмат` if it remains part of the first exchange.

## 5. Proposed Revised Lesson Structure

Do not write final content in this brief. The intended structure for the future revision is:

### Vocabulary

- 4-6 vocabulary or phrase items maximum.
- Prefer phrases that appear directly in the dialogue.
- Consider removing `сен` unless it is needed for a natural first exchange.
- Treat phrase chunks as vocabulary if the learner should memorize them.

### Dialogue

- 1 short natural dialogue.
- 2-4 short turns maximum.
- Should model the exact beginner exchange the learner will practice.
- Should avoid unnatural direct translation from Russian.

### Reading

- Either no reading, or one tiny micro-reading using only the taught words.
- If included, it should not introduce questionable forms like `Мен жакшы.` unless methodist-approved.

### Language Note

- One simple language note, not a heavy grammar explanation.
- Explain only what the learner needs to use the dialogue.
- RU_KY explanation should be concise and practical.

### Practice

3-5 exercises, selected only if appropriate after content revision:

- Multiple choice: recognize a word or phrase meaning.
- Match pairs: match Kyrgyz phrase to RU meaning.
- Fill blank: complete the core response.
- Sentence builder: only if the target phrase has a natural two-part structure.
- Error correction: only if methodist confirms it is appropriate and not too grammar-heavy for the first lesson.
